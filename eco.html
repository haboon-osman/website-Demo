Core Departments in a Bachelor of Economics Program Microeconomics Focuses on individual economic agents, such as households and firms, and their interactions in specific markets. Macroeconomics Deals with the economy as a whole, including topics like inflation, unemployment, and economic growth. Econometrics Involves the application of statistical and mathematical methods to economic data to test hypotheses and forecast future trends. Public Economics Studies government policies and their impact on the economy, including taxation, public expenditure, and regulation. Development Economics Explores economic development processes in low-income countries, addressing issues such as poverty, inequality, and growth. International Economics Examines economic interactions between countries, including trade, international finance, and global markets. Labor Economics Focuses on the functioning and dynamics of labor markets, wage determination, and employment policies. Environmental Economics Analyzes the economic impact of environmental policies, natural resource management, and sustainability. Health Economics Studies economic issues related to health care systems, health behaviors, and health policies. Financial Economics Examines how resources are allocated in financial markets, the behavior of financial institutions, and the valuation of financial assets. Behavioral Economics Integrates insights from psychology into economic models to better understand decision-making processes.
